# Tax Policies

The Tax Policies feature allows a business to define and maintain the tax rules that apply to its products and services. It provides a simple place to register tax names, rates and types so they can be reused consistently across the application.

## How tax policies are defined

When creating a tax policy, the user:

1. Enters a descriptive name (for example, a VAT label or a local tax rule).
2. Sets the numerical rate in percent (0–100).
3. Chooses a tax type from predefined options:
   - **STANDARD** – typical full rate tax.
   - **REDUCED** – reduced rate, e.g., for specific categories.
   - **ZERO** – zero tax rate.
4. Confirms the form to save the policy.
5. Receives a success message if the tax was stored correctly, or an error message if something went wrong.

A reset action is available to clear the form and start again.

## How this feature is used in the business

- When a business needs to add new tax rules, for example:
  - Introducing a new VAT rate.
  - Differentiating between standard and reduced tax categories.
- When existing tax rules must be updated or reviewed.
- When owners and managers want a clear overview of all tax policies used in pricing.

Once defined, these tax policies can be referenced by other parts of the system (such as product and order pricing) to ensure that taxes are applied consistently.

## Who typically uses it

- Business owners and managers who are responsible for pricing and tax compliance.
- Not intended for regular employees or customers, but their orders and bills will reflect the tax rules configured here.
